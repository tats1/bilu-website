How It Works

Engineered for people
who can't stop.

Most focus tools ask you to pause. Bilu works while you work — a barely-visible overlay running silently in the background of your screen, doing the heavy lifting your nervous system never gets a break from.

01

The overlay activates

A semi-transparent orb appears at the edge of your screen — subtle enough to ignore, present enough to work. It bounces left to right while you write, code, trade, or create.

02

Your peripheral vision does the work

You don't need to focus on it. The rhythmic left-right motion is processed by your peripheral visual system, triggering the same neurological pathways activated in clinical EMDR therapy.

03

Binaural audio locks in the effect

An optional 528Hz tone pans in sync with the ball — left ear, right ear, back and forth. This dual-channel bilateral effect deepens the calming response.

04

Heart rate guides the response

When stress spikes are detected, the orb shifts to a breathing-guide pulse — expanding and contracting to coach your nervous system back to baseline without stopping work.

The Science

Not wellness.
Neuroscience.

Bilateral stimulation isn't a wellness trend. It's the core mechanism behind EMDR — one of the most rigorously studied therapies in existence, with over 30 published randomized controlled trials. We took that mechanism and built a passive, always-on tool around it.

🧠

Bilateral stimulation dampens the amygdala

Rhythmic left-right sensory input suppresses excitability in the basolateral nucleus of the amygdala — the brain's primary fear and stress center — through activation of the superior colliculus and mediodorsal thalamus.

Baek et al. (2019) — Systematic review of bilateral stimulation mechanisms
⚡

Proven across 30+ randomized controlled trials

EMDR therapy — built on bilateral stimulation — sits on the same evidence tier as Cognitive Processing Therapy in U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs clinical guidelines. It measurably reduces amygdala reactivity and alters brainwave patterns associated with threat.

VA/DoD Clinical Guidelines (2023) — Wright et al. meta-analysis (2024)
🎵

528Hz — embedded in every session

Our binaural layer uses 528Hz paired with synchronized stereo panning. This dual-channel stimulus engages both hemispheres simultaneously, creating a deeper regulatory effect than visual stimulation alone.

PLOS One fMRI study — alternating auditory stimulation and amygdala response
👁️

Peripheral vision, not focused attention

Unlike traditional EMDR which requires active eye tracking, Bilu works through peripheral vision — meaning you never stop working. The effect happens automatically through your visual system's ambient processing channels.

Stingl et al. (2025) — British Journal of Psychiatry Open
💓

Heart rate-driven adaptive response

When your heart rate rises above your personal baseline, the app enters guided breathing mode. The 4-second inhale, 6-second exhale cycle activates the parasympathetic nervous system and reduces cortisol — while you keep working.

HRV breathing research — parasympathetic nervous system activation
🔄

Passive, not active — the key difference

Every existing bilateral stimulation tool requires a dedicated session. Bilu is the first passive implementation — designed to run continuously in the background, providing cumulative regulatory benefit across an entire workday.

Novel application — continuous peripheral bilateral stimulation during cognitive work
