How It Works

Engineered to sustain
your relentless drive.

Most focus tools ask you to pause. Bilu works while you work — a barely-visible overlay running silently in the background, doing the heavy lifting your nervous system never gets a break from.

01

The overlay activates

A semi-transparent orb appears at the edge of your screen — subtle enough to ignore, present enough to work. It bounces left to right while you write, code, trade, or create.

02

Your peripheral vision does the work

You don't need to focus on it. The rhythmic left-right motion is processed by your peripheral visual system, triggering the same neurological pathways activated in clinical EMDR therapy.

03

Binaural audio locks in the effect

An optional 528Hz tone pans in sync with the ball — left ear, right ear, back and forth. This dual-channel bilateral effect deepens the calming response.

04

Heart rate guides the response

When stress spikes are detected, the orb shifts to a breathing-guide pulse — expanding and contracting to coach your nervous system back to baseline without stopping work.

Burnout Prevention

Your brain
needs a cooling
system.

Burnout isn't caused by working hard. It's caused by your nervous system never getting a chance to regulate — accumulating stress day after day until the system breaks down.

Bilu acts like a passive coolant running in the background. The bilateral stimulus continuously dampens amygdala reactivity, preventing the cortisol accumulation that leads to exhaustion, emotional depletion, and cognitive decline.

Think of it as the difference between a car that runs hot all day and one with a properly functioning cooling system. Same engine. Very different outcome over time.

Real Situations

The moment before
you react.

Most bad decisions don't happen because of bad judgment. They happen in the half-second your nervous system hijacks your thinking. Bilu is built for that exact moment — across every part of life where pressure clouds clarity.

📉
High-Stakes Trading

The chart drops. Your hand is already on the sell button.

A position moves against you fast. Your heart rate spikes, your thinking narrows, and suddenly you're not analyzing — you're reacting. Most regretted trades happen in this exact window, not because the thesis was wrong, but because the nervous system took over before the brain could finish the thought.

With heart rate response active, Bilu detects the spike and shifts into a breathing guide right as the panic starts — giving you a few extra seconds of regulation before you act. Not advice on what to trade. Just space to think clearly before you do.

⚽
Live In-Game Betting

It's the 89th minute. The odds just shifted. You're already typing in a number.

Live betting moves fast, and so does the emotional pull to chase a loss or double down on excitement. The decisions made in that adrenaline spike are rarely the ones people are proud of the next morning.

Calm Mode gives you a manual reset for exactly that moment — no watch required. A few minutes of bilateral pacing and a breathing guide can be the difference between a decision made on logic versus one made on adrenaline.

📚
Deadlines & School

The cursor is blinking. The deadline is in three hours. Your mind is everywhere except the page.

Deadline anxiety doesn't usually look like panic — it looks like avoidance. Refreshing tabs. Reorganizing your desk. Anything except starting. The pressure builds quietly until it's loud.

Bilu runs in the background the entire time you're working, applying continuous low-level regulation instead of waiting for a crisis point. Many users find it lowers the activation energy needed to just start.

🧠
ADHD & Racing Thoughts

Five tabs open. Three half-finished thoughts. None of them landing anywhere.

For many people with ADHD, the challenge isn't a lack of focus — it's too many competing signals at once. A steady, predictable, rhythmic input in the background can act as an anchor point for an otherwise scattered attention system.

Several early users with ADHD report that the consistent left-right motion gives their mind something stable to orient around, similar to how white noise or fidgeting helps some people focus. It's not a substitute for treatment, but it's a tool that fits how their brain already works.

💬
Interviews & Difficult Conversations

You know exactly what you want to say. Your voice isn't cooperating.

High-stakes conversations — job interviews, performance reviews, hard feedback — can trigger the same physiological stress response as physical danger, even though nothing dangerous is actually happening.

A short Calm Mode session before the call, or letting Bilu run during it if you're on a desktop, helps keep your nervous system out of threat mode so you can actually access the thoughts you prepared.

A note on this section: Bilu supports emotional regulation and stress reduction. It does not predict markets, improve odds, or guarantee any financial outcome — and it's not a substitute for professional treatment of ADHD, anxiety, or any clinical condition. What it offers is a steadier nervous system for whatever decision is actually yours to make.
